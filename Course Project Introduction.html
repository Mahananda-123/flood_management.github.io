Problem Definition

"Create a dynamic system that ensures public safety, minimizes property damage, and accelerates post-flood recovery."

Modules of the System

1. Flood Prediction and Early Warning

Users: Disaster management teams, citizens.

Functions:

  • Collection of meteorological, hydrological, and geographical data.
  • Real-time integration of weather and water-level data.
  • AI-based forecasting models using machine learning and time series analysis.

Algorithm: Time Series Analysis, Heap Sort.

Data Structures: Strings, Vectors, Graphs, Heaps.

Outcome: Early and accurate alerts to safeguard lives and mitigate damage.

View Program

2. Flood Evacuation Planning

Users: Authorities, local communities.

Functions:

  • Risk zone prediction to identify high-risk areas.
  • Traffic flow modeling to design efficient evacuation routes.
  • Dynamic evacuation planning based on real-time updates.

Algorithms: Knuth-Morris-Pratt, Dijkstra's, Prim's Algorithm.

Data Structures: Vectors, Strings, Unordered Maps, Structures, Graphs.

Outcome: Safe, efficient, and well-organized evacuations during flood emergencies.

View Program

3. Flood Risk Mapping

Users: Planners, citizens, authorities.

Functions:

  • Identification of flood-prone areas.
  • Visualization of flood depth and risk zones.

Algorithms: Linear Search, String Matching Algorithms.

Data Structures: Vectors, Strings, Unordered Maps, 2D Arrays.

Outcome: Strategic urban development and improved community awareness of risk zones.

View Program

4. Drainage Network Optimization

Users: Engineers, municipal bodies.

Functions:

  • Real-time monitoring of drainage systems.
  • Control and optimization of water flow in urban areas.
  • Efficient planning of drainage system upgrades.

Algorithms: Flow Simulation, Graph Theory.

Data Structures: Graphs, Queues, Strings, 2D Arrays.

Outcome: Reduced waterlogging and more efficient water flow management.
